Frida Snilstveit Hoem

Postdoctoral Researcher

University of Bergen | Bjerknes Centre for Climate Research

I am a paleoceanographer studying how oceans respond to climate change. My work integrates micropaleontology, organic and carbonate geochemistry, and climate model–data synthesis to reconstruct past ocean circulation, productivity, and cryosphere–ocean feedbacks. I currently focus on North Atlantic structure and AMOC stability during past warm periods (mid-Pliocene, M2–KM5c) to better understand future climate tipping points.

Driven by curiosity about our oceans and climate system, my research has taken me across the globe. Along the way, I have developed broad expertise in fieldwork, laboratory analytics, data analysis and visualization, writing, teaching, and public outreach. I synthesize research on changing oceans in a warming world, translating complex science into insights that inform public understanding and policy decisions.

Work Experience

2023-present
Postdoc, University of Bergen & Bjerknes Centre for Climate Research

As a Marie Curie "Shaping European Research Leaders for Marine Sustainability (SEAS)" research fellow I research the Atlantic meridional overturning circulation (AMOC) stability during the warm world analogue, The Pliocene; the most recent epoch CO2 was as high as current and predicted future levels. More specifically I study fossil remains of foraminifera shells and their stable isotope composition (δ¹³C, δ¹⁸O and δ¹⁵N) in Pliocene marine sediment deposits to reconstruct Southern Ocean and North Atlantic structure, nutrient and oxygen availability and primary productivity in comparison with available multiproxy data and model simulations to elucidate the interactions between climate, oceanographic and biological processes.

2022-2023
Postdoc, Utrecht University, Marine Palynology and Paleoceanography

As a postdoctoral Researcher within the ERC-funded “OceaNice” project, I worked on palynological and organic geochemical proxy development and led international model–data collaborations on past Southern Ocean climate dynamics.

2017-2022
PhD, Utrecht University, Marine Palynology and Paleoceanography

Project: “The role of warm oceans in past Antarctic ice-sheet variability”
Reconstructing past sea-ice cover, frontal systems, productivity, ocean structure, and temperatures using microfossil (Dinoflagellate cysts) and organic biomarker (TEX86, UK37) records from Southern Ocean marine sedimentary records coupled with climate/ocean modelling. Improved Southern Ocean biostratigraphy.
